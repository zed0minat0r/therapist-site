## SECTION-BY-SECTION BREAKDOWN

### 1. Hero — 8.0 / 10
The green pulsing dot badge "Currently accepting new clients" is the single most impactful change in this cycle. For a prospective patient arriving at 10pm, that one line removes the primary ambiguity: is this practice even open? The dot-pulse animation is exactly the right weight — present without being loud. The phone icon on the tap-to-call number makes the affordance immediately obvious on mobile. The hero stat bar, botanical parallax, and headline copy remain strong. This section now does everything it should.

**Remaining gap:** The hero CTA reads "Schedule a Consultation" while the mid-page CTA section reads "Book a Free Consultation." The word "free" is doing real conversion work and has disappeared from the primary CTA. A visitor who clicks the hero button and lands on the form sees "Book a Free Consultation" on the submit button — the journey is internally consistent from CTA section onward, but the hero-to-form gap remains.

### 2. Approach — 7.5 / 10
Unchanged. The pillar layout, count-up animation, terracotta h3 accent on Pillar 03, and Carl Rogers pull-quote are all still present and correct. No regressions.

**Remaining gap:** The Carl Rogers quote is slightly academic for a first-time visitor who may be in distress. It is fine for a practice that wants to signal intellectual rigour, but a warmer, less clinical alternative — something that speaks to the experience of being heard, rather than the theory of acceptance — would connect more viscerally with the anxious browser.

### 3. Services — 7.7 / 10
The group therapy description is now substantive: "8-10 weeks — enquire for current schedules and openings." This replaces an empty gesture with a real signal. A prospective group therapy client can now make a preliminary judgement about fit. The pricing note ("sliding scale available") remains correctly placed. The editorial list format avoids the card-grid cliche.

**Remaining gap:** The word "enquire" is British English. In Media, PA, the expected spelling is "inquire." This is a minor signal that the copy was not locally reviewed — the sort of thing a sharp-eyed prospective client will notice, even if they cannot name why it feels slightly off.

### 4. Specialties — 7.8 / 10
The exit CTA — "Don't see your concern listed? Reach out — chances are we can help." — now appears below the constellation, which is where the eye exits the section. The terracotta link is correctly styled. This was a specific gap at 7.6 and it has been closed correctly. The staggered animation and alternating row entrance remain the most visually distinctive scroll moment on the site.

### 5. About — 7.5 / 10
No changes in this cycle. The prose is strong. The FAQ answers the right questions. The Penn MSW credential placement remains correct. The address links to Google Maps.

**Remaining gap:** The About section is entirely text-based. A single environmental photograph — Amy's office, a window, a bookshelf, anything that grounds the physical space — would humanise the practice in a way that 400 words of well-written bio copy cannot. This is the largest remaining gap on the site and is not easily fixed without a real photograph.

### 6. Testimonials — 7.8 / 10
The fourth testimonial (D.L., Bryn Mawr PA) is exactly right: an adult individual client, nervous about starting therapy, describing the first session as feeling cared for rather than processed. This closes the gap identified at 7.6, where all three existing quotes described family and child outcomes. The attribution pattern (initials, town, PA) is consistent. "Shared with permission" is correctly styled. Four testimonials is a substantive enough set for a practice of this size.

**Remaining gap:** None significant at this count. A fifth testimonial from a couples client would complete the service coverage, but this is a low-priority refinement, not a gap.

### 7. CTA Section — 7.6 / 10
The terracotta background, contrast, and headline copy remain strong. The form submit button now reads "Book a Free Consultation" — consistent with this section's language.

**Remaining gap:** The hero CTA reads "Schedule a Consultation" (no "free"). The journey from hero button to form is internally inconsistent on the question of cost. Unifying to "Book a Free Consultation" at every call-to-action would close this.

### 8. Contact Form — 7.8 / 10
The "Not sure yet" card is now correctly handled on mobile: a 2-column CSS grid produces clean 2-2-1 rows, with "Not sure yet" spanning full width centered. The submit button now reads "Book a Free Consultation" throughout, matching the CTA section above. The inline validation and privacy reassurance note remain correct.

### 9. Mobile UX at 375px — 7.8 / 10
The service card orphan has been fixed cleanly. The availability badge centres correctly on mobile with the reduced-motion fallback confirmed. Tap targets are confirmed at 44px throughout. Centre-alignment is consistent. The phone icon on the hero tap-to-call is the right affordance for a mobile-first visitor.

**Remaining gap:** The hero button ("Schedule a Consultation") and the phone number are stacked vertically on mobile. The phone number's styling is good — icon, underline, correct size — but it sits slightly closer to the button than to the viewport edge, giving the impression that they are grouped. A small additional margin-top between the button and the phone link would clarify that these are two separate actions.

### 10. Technical and SEO — 7.5 / 10
No regressions. Schema markup, WebP hero, fetchpriority, prefers-reduced-motion, Intersection Observer cleanup all confirmed. No new technical debt introduced.

## TOP 3 PRIORITIES

### PRIORITY 1: Fix the hero CTA copy to include "free"
"Schedule a Consultation" in the hero should become "Book a Free Consultation" to match the mid-page CTA and the form submit. The word "free" is the highest-conversion word on the contact journey and it currently disappears at the first touchpoint. This is a one-word change in the HTML.

### PRIORITY 2: Add environmental photography to the About section
The About section is entirely text. A single photograph — the office interior, a detail of the space, even a well-composed shot of the building entrance — would close the largest humanisation gap remaining on the site. Text alone, however well-written, cannot tell a nervous first-time visitor what it feels like to walk through the door. This requires a real photograph, which may require Amy's involvement.

### PRIORITY 3: Fix "enquire" to "inquire" in the Group Therapy description
"Enquire for current schedules" is British English in a Philadelphia context. The copy was otherwise locally reviewed. This single word is a small but genuine signal that the site was not written by someone from the area. Fix it in the Group Therapy service description.

## CEILING ASSESSMENT

This site is approaching its realistic ceiling as a static single-page build. The foundational decisions — Cormorant Garamond + DM Sans, forest/parchment/terracotta palette, editorial list layout, animated specialty constellation, botanical parallax — were correct and are executing well. The site is now clearly better than most therapists in suburban Philadelphia and the surrounding Main Line.

The next score threshold (8.0+) requires either: (a) a real photograph of Amy or the office that humanises the practice in a way copy cannot, or (b) a structural addition such as a blog, FAQ expansion, or insurance detail page that gives anxious researchers more to read before committing to contact. Both require content from Amy that does not yet exist on the site.

Short of those, the site is in a very good place. Further CSS or copy polish is subject to diminishing returns. The site is not approaching 8.5+ territory on this format.
