About the Practice

A small team.
Real experience.
Deep care.

We built this practice on a simple belief: that good therapy is not a transaction. It is a relationship. The people who walk through our door — children, teens, adults, couples, whole families — deserve a space that feels honest, unhurried, and genuinely safe.

This is a small practice by design. We stay that way because caseload size affects care quality. We collaborate closely with your other providers — psychiatrists, pediatricians, school counselors — because the best outcomes happen when everyone is aligned around the same person.

Amy Fantalis, LCSW

Licensed Clinical Social Worker — 17+ years in practice

I started this practice in 2007 because I believed — and still believe — that every person deserves a space where they can say anything without judgment, and actually feel heard. After years working in community mental health and outpatient counseling, I wanted to build something different: a small, close-knit practice where relationships matter more than caseloads.

Trained in CBT, DBT, and trauma-informed care, I work with individuals, families, and couples across the full arc of life — from childhood through adulthood. I hold a Master of Social Work from the University of Pennsylvania.

Do you accept insurance?
We are out-of-network with most insurers. Many clients are reimbursed through out-of-network benefits or HSA/FSA accounts. We’re happy to provide a superbill. Sliding scale available — please ask.
Is telehealth available?
Yes. We offer secure video sessions for clients across Pennsylvania. Many clients alternate between in-person and telehealth depending on their week.
How long are sessions?
Standard sessions are 50 minutes. Extended 80-minute sessions are available for couples and family work, or when a client needs more time to process.
